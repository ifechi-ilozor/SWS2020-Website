Programming

Learn more about the initiatives we run at Brown SWS and how we can add value to your life.

Series, Workshops & Other Opportunities

Series

Fall Lecture Series

Every single fall semester, SWS hosts a comprehensive multi-week seminar series in which students, mainly freshman and sophomores but we've had upperclassmen in attendance as well, learn about a breadth of essential finance topics. These lectures are led by experienced upperclassmen who've had work experience at top firms on the Street, highly-rated Economics professors and successful industry professionals. While participants learn a breadth of topics, we delve deep in these lectures and keep things engaging. Topics you can expect to learn are personal investing, sourcing investment ideas, and understanding the 3 financial statements and 10-K/10-Q reports.

Research Committee

Every spring semester, SWS hosts an intensive Research Committee for members who are looking to gain more hands-on experience with finance. This program is particularly recommended for those who completed the Fall Lecture Series. Throughout the term, we hold pitching cycles, where analysts learn essentials about pitching (format, conventions, analysis) and work in groups to pitch a stock from a certain industry. Exciting upcoming additions to Research Committee: SWS is looking to hold a more advanced Research Committee year-round for analysts who demonstrate exceptional research and pitching skills. The benefits of this group include priority for competition sponsorships, research publishment in an intercollegiate research library used by non-institutional and institutional investors, and an opportunity to manage real funds.

Workshops

In addition to our main initiatives, throughout the year, we run several workshops for further member enrichment. One of our most popular is Interview Prep. We hold Interview Prep workshops throughout the year led by upperclassmen who've interned at companies like Goldman Sachs, J.P. Morgan, McKinsey & Company, and more. In these sessions, we give insider tips and career help that's not common in other info sessions or workshops. Some other events we've held in the past are internship panels, crash courses with professors, collaborations with other finance clubs, as well as some social networking events (the lovely National Executive Director sometimes drops by too!).

Other Opportunities

While education is definitely our main focus at SWS, we don't ignore the fact that many of our members are aiming for successful careers in the financial services, and we are dedicated to propelling them forward. We believe that the knowledge we provide in our series and workshops is something that extensively prepares our members for interviews and bolsters their resumes, and we extend several opportunities for members to showcase that work. We co-host info sessions with competitive companies for our members and send out exclusive programs on our mailing list. We sponsor members to go to conferences and competitions such as the William & Mary Stock Pitch Competition, the Women in Investing Summit, and the Intercollegiate Business Conference. National members also have the chance to go to The SWS National Conference, The National Summit, and The National Stock Pitch Competition.
