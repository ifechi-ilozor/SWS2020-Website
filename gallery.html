SWS is a club for everyone, no matter your experience level. We'd love to have you!

Membership Tiers

All SWS Membership is Free. We Value Your Committment to SWS Over All Else.

We are committed to being accessible to the entire Brown community. College is expensive. If you have money left over from tuition, please save it for a rainy day or better yet, invest it. Vertical progression in this organization is based solely off of merit and committment.

General Member

You are on our mailing list (subscribe below!) so you regularly receive updates on Brown SWS, our currently running programs as well as professional opportunities. You may have attended to a couple of our workshops, kickoffs and/or showcases. You have access to all our series, events and collaborations.

National Member

You attended at least 80% of the Fall Lecture Series. One of our board members will have reached out to you at the end of the fall semester to have you register for National Membership. Note: If you are a National Member, you are automatically an Associate so you also have all of those benefits. You have access to all series, events, and collaborations plus several benefits afforded by the national chapter:

  • Eligible to apply for The National Conference, Corporate Treks, Industry Conferences & the Warren Buffett Trip
  • Resume gets put in the National Resume Book (seen by companies such as Point72, Fidelity Investments and TD Ameritrade)
  • Pre-selected recognition for programs with National sponsors and partners

Associate

You have attended at least 80% of the Fall Lecture Series, and/or you have attended 4 workshops in one semester, and/or you are an analyst in the Research Committee. One of our board members will have reached out to you at the end of the semester to inform you. You have access to all series, events, and collaborations plus several benefits:

  • Exclusive recruiting resources and referrals gathered from alumni
  • Priority for Executive Board applications
  • Priority for external experiences
  • Opportunity to be selected for the advanced Research Committee
